Step 1: Problem

Identify challenges and design a problem statement

PHASE 1

★ Select Your Focus SDG

Choose one SDG that your project will address. This will guide all your responses.

🤝 Social Causes (SDG 1–10)

🌍 Environmental Causes (SDG 11–15)

🏛️ Governance & Global (SDG 16–17)

✅ Selected SDG:

1 Current Affairs Connection

Identify a current global issue related to your chosen SDG. Use the articles resource above.

2 Local Context

How does this global issue manifest locally in your city or country?

3 Root Causes

What are the underlying causes of this problem? Think beyond surface-level symptoms.

4 Stakeholders

Who is affected by this problem? Who has the power to change it?

★ Problem Statement

Synthesise your research into a clear, focused problem statement using the format: "How might we [action] for [target group] so that [outcome]?"

📚 Resources: 🎨 IDEO Design Kit 🎤 TED: Social Change 🏫 GC Microsite

Step 2: Solution

Brainstorm, evaluate and design your solution

PHASE 2

1 Brainstorm Solutions

Generate at least 3 possible solutions. Think creatively — no idea is too wild at this stage!

2 Evaluate & Select

Evaluate your ideas against feasibility, impact, and innovation. Which solution will you pursue and why?

3 Solution Design

Describe your chosen solution in detail. What does it look like? How does it work? Who delivers it?

4 Theory of Change

Complete the logic chain: If we do X, then Y will happen, which will lead to Z outcome.

★ Risks & Mitigation

What could go wrong? How will you address these challenges?

📚 Resources: 🎨 Canva 🌐 Global Goals 🏫 GC Microsite

Step 3: Action

Plan and document your action steps

PHASE 3

1 Action Plan

Break your solution into concrete, time-bound action steps. Who does what, and by when?

2 Resources Needed

What people, materials, funding, or partnerships do you need to execute your plan?

3 Measuring Impact

How will you know your solution is working? Define 2–3 measurable indicators of success.

★ Team Reflection

Reflect on your team's journey. What did you learn? What would you do differently?

📚 Resources: 🎨 Canva Slides 🎤 TED: Great Talks 🏫 GC Microsite

Step 4: Present

Craft and rehearse your presentation

PHASE 4

1 Pitch Structure

Outline your presentation structure. A strong pitch covers: hook, problem, solution, evidence, call to action.

2 Storytelling Element

Great presentations use stories to connect emotionally. Describe a real or hypothetical story of someone affected by your issue.

3 Data & Evidence

What statistics, research, or evidence will you use to support your argument? Cite your sources.

4 Anticipated Q&A

What tough questions might judges or the audience ask? Prepare your answers in advance.

★ Final Summary Statement

Write a compelling 3–5 sentence summary of your entire project. This is your elevator pitch.
